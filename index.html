About the trip

The Vargas Island Expedition is a five-day stand-up paddleboard touring training course based around Vargas Island, just north of Tofino. The official description highlights coastal decision-making, weather and swell reading, route planning, efficient paddling, safety and rescue, and group management — while travelling through Clayoquot Sound’s outer coast.

When conditions allow, the group aims for a full circumnavigation of Vargas Island in the traditional waters of the Tla-o-qui-aht First Nation — white-sand beaches, exposed swell, rocky headlands, and sheltered inlets in one compact classroom.

Participants carry their own gear and pack their own food. Daily distances are often roughly 10–20 km, depending on weather and sea state, with a fully loaded touring board.

Land and marine etiquette

Paddling around Vargas Island crosses stewarded territory. Third-party sources such as BC Marine Trails describe Ahousaht Nation protocol fees for camping and day use, where to buy passes in Tofino, and how the Guardian program may work in summer. Always follow your guide’s briefing and the latest local rules — they change over time.

Verify with the organizer. Meeting times, fees, and packing notes below reflect a pre-trip message for this departure. They can change — treat email from Norm Hann Expeditions as the source of truth.

Food

You bring and pack your own food. Plan filling, nutritious meals for paddling days. You need food from lunch and snacks on Day 1 through lunch on Day 5. Freeze-dried meals are light and work well for SUP camping. Pack snacks and bars within easy reach in case days run long.

Buying or borrowing gear

For boards, paddles, dry bags, or other purchases, email explore@normhann.com. The Norm Hann Expeditions shop may offer discount codes — ask when you inquire.

Suggested packing list

June on the west coast can be warm, but the ocean and rainforest stay cool. Heavy rain and fog are possible any week. Bring immersion-ready layers, a warm sleeping bag, and expect mixed weather.

Paddling equipment

  • Inflatable or hard touring board suited to ocean touring — minimum 12′6″ with front and rear tie-downs
  • Paddle (adjustable is fine)
  • PFD — jacket style (no waist-belt PFDs)
  • Coil or quick-release touring leash
  • Dry bags for gear

Safety and navigation

  • Chart #3673
  • Chart case
  • Compass
  • Communication — phone and/or VHF if you have one
  • Small first aid kit

What to wear for paddling (pick an approach)

  • Option 1: Drysuit
  • Option 2: Waterproof paddling pants and jacket
  • Option 3: Farmer Jane/John wetsuit with paddling top
  • Neoprene booties with a solid sole, or a water shoe such as Astral Hiyak
  • Fleece or synthetic hooded mid-layer
  • Waterproof jacket and pants (as needed with your option)
  • Warm mid-layer or down jacket (in camp / breaks)
  • Baseball cap and toque
  • Paddling gloves for cool days or sensitive hands

Other clothing

  • Synthetic shorts
  • Quick-dry pants
  • Two long-sleeve shirts
  • Two short-sleeve shirts
  • One wool sweater
  • Two pairs wool or synthetic socks
  • Two sets long underwear
  • Two to three pairs underwear

Personal items

  • Toiletries
  • Sunscreen
  • Sunglasses with retainer
  • Prescription glasses or contacts, plus spares
  • Wet wipes
  • Synthetic camp towel
  • Medications plus spare supply
  • Insect repellent
  • Book
  • Headlamp and batteries
  • Camera (optional), extra cards and batteries
  • 1 L water bottle
  • Thermos

Camping gear

  • Lightweight 1–2 person tent
  • Compressible three-season sleeping bag
  • Sleeping pad or Therm-a-Rest
  • Small packable pillow
  • Small stove — Jetboil, MSR WhisperLite, or similar
  • Fuel — about 500 ml butane or 1 L white gas (match your stove)
  • Cooking pot if not using Jetboil
  • Knife, fork, spoon, mug
  • ~10 L dromedary bag plus water treatment (e.g. Pristine or Aquatabs)

Cancellation (from public listing)

The WeTravel listing describes a $300 non-refundable deposit at booking, full payment about 60 days before the start, and refund rules tied to that window. Buy travel insurance if you want coverage beyond the operator’s policy — confirm wording on your invoice.
